## Context

Researched G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Reddit, and hands-on reviews of Planoly, Plann, Planable, Publer, and Buffer to validate our product thesis and refine the roadmap.

## What happened?

Every social media scheduler on the market is **calendar-first, not capture-first**. The most damning evidence came from a Planable review on Capterra: "I wish there was a better way to add ideas for future posts or make notes of holidays/events." That's exactly our product.

Planoly has major reliability issues (posts disappearing, no support). Plann has no X/Twitter support and basic AI. Reddit shows creators still using spreadsheets because Notion "can't handle images in calendar views."

## What I learned

The competitor landscape is all execution (scheduling + posting). Zero tools handle ideation + capture + listening in one flow. Our positioning of "inbox first, calendar second" has no direct competitor. The gap between inspiration and planning is real and unserved.

## What should change?

- [ ] Move Telegram bot earlier in the roadmap (capture IS the product)
- [ ] Make AI enrichment a Phase 2 priority (leapfrog competitors' "basic AI")
- [ ] Design dashboard mobile-first from the start
- [ ] Structure free tier around captured ideas, not scheduled posts
